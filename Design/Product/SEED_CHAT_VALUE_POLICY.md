# Seed Chat Value Policy

Status: confirmed product direction. This policy governs human employees,
prepared text, AI assistance, and robot conversations conducted through
internally classified seed profiles.

## 1. Purpose
Seed conversations must provide real conversational value to customers. The customer should leave a conversation feeling heard, respected, lighter, calmer, happier, amused, affectionately engaged, or interested in talking again.

Continued conversation must result from quality and mutual interest, never from deception, emotional pressure, manufactured urgency, or pressure to spend.

## 2. Value Pillars

### Feeling Heard
- Notice the customer's stated mood and interests.
- Respond to the substance of the customer's message.
- Ask relevant follow-up questions.
- Remember approved non-sensitive preferences within the conversation.
- Reflect feelings without pretending to diagnose the customer.

Desired outcome:
The customer feels that the conversation was attentive rather than generic.

### Love And Affection
- Offer warmth, appreciation, respectful compliments, and consensual romance.
- Let affection develop gradually according to the relationship stage.
- Distinguish playful romance from real-world promises.
- Respect rejection, hesitation, boundaries, and topic changes immediately.

Desired outcome:
The customer experiences safe romantic enjoyment without fabricated offline
promises, identity claims, or pressure to spend.

### Mood Support
- Acknowledge difficult feelings with care.
- Use gentle questions, encouragement, and perspective when appropriate.
- Celebrate positive news and personal progress.
- Allow the customer to be quiet, change the subject, or end the conversation.

Desired outcome:
The customer feels emotionally supported, without the seed acting as a therapist or sole source of support.

### Relaxation
- Offer calm, low-pressure conversation.
- Use comfortable subjects such as music, food, nature, travel ideas, hobbies, and pleasant memories.
- Match the customer's pace and avoid excessive questions.
- Do not introduce conflict, spending prompts, or intimate escalation when the customer wants to relax.

Desired outcome:
The conversation helps the customer unwind.

### Happiness And Positivity
- Share encouragement, appreciation, playful imagination, and positive topics.
- Recognize small wins without forced cheerfulness.
- Match the customer's emotional state rather than dismissing sadness.
- Avoid exaggerated praise that feels scripted or manipulative.

Desired outcome:
The customer finishes in a better or more hopeful mood when appropriate.

### Fun And Humor
- Use light jokes, playful questions, wordplay, amusing hypotheticals, and friendly teasing.
- Adapt humor to the customer's language, culture, mood, and relationship stage.
- Never joke about trauma, protected characteristics, appearance, disability, consent, financial status, or customer vulnerability.
- Stop teasing when it is not welcomed.
- Do not claim a fabricated real-world funny experience as something that actually happened to the seed.

Desired outcome:
The customer smiles or laughs without feeling targeted or embarrassed.

### Natural Continuity
- Build on earlier topics instead of repeatedly restarting.
- Balance questions, responses, and voluntary topic sharing.
- Offer topic choices when a conversation slows.
- Use callbacks to interests the customer has willingly shared.
- End conversations warmly when the customer wants to leave.

Desired outcome:
The customer is willing to keep talking because the exchange remains interesting and comfortable.

## 3. Conversation Pattern
A good response generally contains two or three of these elements:
1. Acknowledge the customer's message or mood.
2. Add a relevant thought, playful idea, or supportive response.
3. Ask one natural, open question or offer a small choice of topics.

This is guidance, not a rigid template. Employees and automated systems must vary structure and avoid interrogation-style strings of questions.

## 4. Mood-Adaptive Behavior

### Customer Is Happy
- Celebrate with them.
- Invite them to share what made the moment good.
- Use playful or energetic conversation if welcomed.

### Customer Is Sad Or Lonely
- Acknowledge the feeling.
- Listen before trying to improve the mood.
- Avoid romantic escalation, spending prompts, or exclusivity language.
- Escalate safety concerns according to policy.

### Customer Is Stressed
- Slow the pace.
- Keep responses clear and calm.
- Offer a lighter topic without forcing a change.

### Customer Is Bored
- Offer playful choices, a short game, a hypothetical question, humor, or a fresh approved topic.
- Do not create false emergencies or romantic jealousy to regain attention.

### Customer Is Romantic
- Match the tone only within consent and relationship-stage rules.
- Keep the virtual/company-operated identity clear.
- Do not promise exclusivity, marriage, travel, or an offline meeting.

### Customer Wants To End
- Respect the decision immediately.
- Use a brief, warm goodbye.
- Do not use guilt, fear, repeated messages, or expiring-affection claims to keep the customer present.

## 5. Continuity And Memory
Permitted conversational memory may include:
- Preferred name
- Approved interests and hobbies
- Preferred conversation topics and tone
- Topics the customer does not want
- Consent state
- Previous conversation summary
- Important non-sensitive details voluntarily shared

Do not infer or retain sensitive facts merely to increase engagement. Customers need controls appropriate to the privacy policy for conversational memory, correction, and deletion.

Every callback must be truthful. A seed cannot claim to personally remember an event unless the system actually retained the relevant approved conversation context.

## 6. Disallowed Engagement Methods
Seed operators and automated systems must not:
- Claim the seed is a real independent person
- Say or imply that the seed is physically waiting nearby
- Promise a meeting, marriage, relocation, or exclusive relationship
- Tell a customer that purchasing credits or gifts proves love
- Delay a meaningful reply merely to increase message spending
- Intentionally divide one answer into unnecessary paid messages
- Create jealousy, guilt, fear, abandonment, or urgency to prolong chat
- Claim “you are all I have” or “you only need me”
- Discourage real relationships, family, friends, or professional support
- Exploit loneliness, grief, intoxication, disability, cognitive impairment, or financial distress
- Repeatedly re-engage a customer who has asked to stop
- Hide the price or other required paid-action information

## 7. Special Safety Boundaries
- Self-harm, abuse, exploitation, threats, coercion, and immediate danger follow the mandatory safety workflow.
- Medical, legal, financial, and mental-health topics must not be handled as professional advice.
- Adult intimate conversation requires verified adult eligibility, affirmative consent, and immediate respect for withdrawal.
- Customers showing distress or unusual spending must not receive spending encouragement.
- Sensitive situations transfer to an appropriately trained human when required.

## 8. Prepared Text And AI Requirements
Every suggested response should support at least one value goal:
- Heard
- Affection
- Supported
- Relaxed
- Happier
- Amused
- Curious to continue
- Comfortably concluded

Prepared text and AI assistance must:
- Use current conversation context
- Match customer mood and consent
- Avoid repeating recent phrases
- Avoid generic compliments unrelated to the customer
- Avoid invented personal experiences
- Avoid optimizing for credits, gifts, or message count
- Permit employee editing before sending

Robot auto-send requires separate approval and must be evaluated against this policy.

## 9. Employee Guidance
Employees should:
- Read the recent conversation before responding.
- Prioritize relevance over speed.
- Personalize approved suggestions.
- Use humor carefully and naturally.
- Allow pauses and endings.
- Mark safety, consent, or profile-integrity concerns promptly.

Employee quality evaluation must not reward spending alone. It should include:
- Customer satisfaction
- Relevance and warmth
- Respect for consent and boundaries
- Profile-integrity compliance
- Safety handling
- Low complaint and block rates
- Conversation variety
- Appropriate endings

## 10. Quality Measures
Measure value without treating continued paid chat as the only success:
- Post-conversation satisfaction
- “I felt heard” response
- Mood before/after, when the customer voluntarily supplies it
- Positive reaction and humor response
- Natural return-conversation rate
- Repeated-text rate
- Customer-initiated topic continuation
- Graceful-ending compliance
- Profile-presentation complaint rate
- Block, report, pressure, and manipulation complaints
- Safety-transfer correctness

Do not use revenue, gifts, number of paid messages, or session length as the sole conversation-quality metric.

## 11. Review Standard
A reviewer should be able to answer yes to these questions:
1. Did the response address what the customer actually said?
2. Did it provide warmth, interest, support, relaxation, happiness, or appropriate humor?
3. Was the profile's virtual identity represented truthfully?
4. Were consent, mood, and boundaries respected?
5. Would the response still be acceptable if no further purchase resulted?

If the fifth answer is no, the response likely prioritizes monetization over customer value and must be revised.
