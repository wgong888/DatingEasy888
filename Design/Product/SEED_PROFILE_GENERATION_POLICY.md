# Seed Profile Generation Policy

Status: approved design direction; legal, model-license, and moderation review are still required before generation begins.

## 1. Purpose
Generate realistic, varied, internally classified company-operated profiles
without copying, impersonating, or exploiting real people.

Every seed profile must be:
- Created from scratch or from material the company has explicit commercial rights to use
- Clearly labeled as AI-generated and company-operated
- Traceable to its generation source, policy version, and human approval
- Suitable for an 18+ platform without appearing to depict a minor
- Distinct from real members, public figures, employees, and other seed profiles

## 2. Prohibited Source Method
DatingEasy888 must not:
- Copy a profile from another dating or adult-social website
- Download or reuse another person's photos without explicit commercial rights and model consent
- Slightly edit a real person's face, body, background, clothing, name, age, location, or biography to create a seed
- Paraphrase another person's biography while preserving its recognizable expression or personal story
- Scrape profiles, photos, messages, or personal data for seed generation
- Impersonate a real person or make explicit false claims about an offline
  identity, physical presence, or real-world availability

Changing three photos, a name, age by three years, location, or the first sentence does not make a copied identity original or safe.

## 3. Permitted Market Research
The team may review established adult-social or dating products only for high-level product research, subject to their terms and legal review.

Permitted observations:
- Common profile-field categories
- General biography length and tone
- Broad interest taxonomies
- Common photo compositions
- Search and discovery patterns
- Profile-presentation and safety approaches

Research output must be aggregate design notes. It must not contain downloaded profiles, identifiable photos, copied biographies, usernames, or links used as generation inputs.

## 4. Approved Source Options

### Preferred: Fully Synthetic
- Generate character specifications and photos using commercially approved AI services or company-controlled models.
- Prompts must not name or request resemblance to a real person, celebrity, customer, or employee.
- The resulting identity must not intentionally reproduce a known person's likeness.

### Licensed Human Models
May be used only when:
- The company has a written commercial license.
- The model release expressly permits dating/adult-social profile use.
- The permitted duration, geography, edits, AI transformations, and revocation process are documented.
- The profile remains labeled as company-operated; the model is not represented as an available member.

### Commissioned Content
Photographers, writers, and performers must assign or license the required commercial rights in writing. Consent to one photograph or production does not automatically authorize creation of a dating persona.

## 5. Generation Workflow

### Step 1: Batch Plan
Define the approved distribution before generating profiles:
- Country, state, and city
- Age band
- Gender and looking-for values
- Interests, lifestyle, work-field category, and personality
- Language
- Accessibility and diversity goals

The distribution must follow product policy and must not rely on copied individuals.

### Step 2: Character Specification
Create an original structured character specification from approved vocabularies and combinations.

Do not include:
- Real employer or exact workplace
- Exact home address
- Real phone number, email, or social account
- Government identifier
- Claim of verification as a real independent member
- Fictitious history designed to support false offline identity claims

### Step 3: Name
- Use a fictional display name generated independently from approved name data.
- Do not copy a source profile's name or use a public figure's name.
- Avoid a full name combined with details that could identify a real local person.
- Check for duplicates within the platform.

### Step 4: Age
- Select age independently from the approved demographic plan.
- Do not derive it by adding or subtracting years from a real person's age.
- Seed profiles should be at least 21 years old even though platform eligibility begins at 18.
- Generated photos and biography must be reasonably consistent with the stated age.
- Any profile that could reasonably appear underage is rejected.

### Step 5: Location
- Assign country, state, and city from the approved location catalog.
- Do not move a copied person to a different location.
- Do not provide a street address, exact workplace, or precise real-time location.
- The profile must not imply that the virtual character can meet locally as an independent person.

### Step 6: Biography
- Generate the entire biography from scratch.
- The first sentence must be original and materially different from other seed openings.
- Target length: 80-180 words.
- Hard maximum: fewer than 500 words and no more than 4,000 characters.
- Use a readable, natural tone consistent with the character specification.
- Do not copy or closely paraphrase another person's wording, life story, trauma, relationship history, or distinctive facts.
- Do not pressure the customer to buy credits, send gifts, or continue paying.
- Do not promise an offline meeting.

### Step 7: Photos
Each seed profile initially receives three approved photos:
1. Clear primary portrait
2. Everyday activity or interest
3. Different setting or clothing while preserving the same synthetic identity

Photo rules:
- All three photos must depict the same generated or licensed identity.
- Apparent age, face, body characteristics, and biography must be consistent.
- Profile/discovery photos must be non-explicit.
- No copied background containing another person's home, workplace, children, documents, license plate, or identifying information.
- No celebrity, public figure, customer, employee, or recognizable real-person resemblance.
- Approved publication derivatives use JPG or PNG only.
- Convert other safely readable generated source formats to PNG when
  transparency is required and JPG otherwise.
- Store original generation metadata and approved JPG/PNG derivatives.
- Human moderation is required before publication.

### Step 8: Originality And Safety Review
Before approval:
- Check against internal seed photos and biographies for duplicates and near-duplicates.
- Check names and identity traits for accidental public-figure or employee impersonation.
- Check all photos for adult appearance and prohibited content.
- Check text for copied passages, personal data, manipulation, and prohibited claims.
- Review consistency across name, age, location, interests, biography, and photos.

Do not build a scraped face database to perform these checks.

### Step 9: Human Approval
A trained reviewer must approve:
- Identity originality
- Three-photo consistency
- Adult appearance
- Biography quality and length
- Location and demographic consistency
- Profile-presentation readiness
- Rights/provenance record

The generator cannot approve its own output.

### Step 10: Publication And Profile Presentation
- Customer-facing cards, profiles, chats, and transaction screens use the same
  presentation structure as other customer profiles.
- Customer APIs and screens omit internal real, seed, and robot classification.
- Exact credit costs remain visible before every paid action.
- Internal classification, provenance, generation details, and operator history
  remain available only to authorized administrators, services, and auditors.
- Publication remains blocked until legal, consumer-protection,
  payment-provider, and marketplace review approves this presentation model.

## 6. Uniqueness Rules
- No duplicate primary photo.
- No reused three-photo set.
- No identical biography.
- No repeated first sentence within the active seed catalog.
- Limit repeated sentence patterns and interest combinations.
- Measure near-duplicate text and internal visual similarity before publication.
- A seed identity cannot be recycled as a different person after retirement.

## 7. Provenance Record
For each seed profile, retain:
- Seed customer ID and generation batch
- Character-specification version
- Text and image generation provider/model versions
- Prompt-policy version and generation timestamps
- Asset source type: synthetic, licensed model, or commissioned
- License/model-release reference where applicable
- Originality and safety check results
- Reviewer, approval time, and policy version
- Published profile-presentation policy version
- Revision and retirement history

Prompts and provenance are restricted internal data and must not contain copied personal profiles.

## 8. Revision And Takedown
- Complaints that a seed resembles a real person receive priority review.
- The profile and all associated photos can be disabled immediately.
- Preserve investigation evidence under restricted access.
- Confirm rights and originality before restoration.
- Retired seed identities cannot continue initiating or receiving paid interactions.

## 9. Quality Sampling
Before deployment:
- Review every seed profile individually.
- Audit each generation batch for demographic distribution and repetitive patterns.
- Test customers' understanding that profiles are virtual and company-operated.
- Run a smaller pilot catalog before generating profiles at full scale.

After launch:
- Sample active profiles monthly.
- Re-review after any photo, biography, age, or location change.
- Monitor complaints about resemblance, copied content, misleading identity,
  or profile presentation.

## 10. Governing Principle
Market research may inspire general categories and product patterns. It must never become a source for copying real people's identities. DatingEasy888 seed profiles are original virtual characters, not altered replicas.
