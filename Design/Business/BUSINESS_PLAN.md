# DatingEasy888 Business Plan

Version: planning draft 1.0
Date: June 10, 2026
Planning horizon: Arfa review through controlled public launch

## 1. Executive Summary

DatingEasy888 is a proposed mobile-first social companionship and dating
service for adults. It combines real-member discovery with clearly identified
virtual profiles operated by AI or company employees. The product aims to make
online conversation easier, more responsive, and safer than a generic swipe
application.

The business should not compete immediately as a broad national dating network.
It should validate one customer segment, one geography for real-member
discovery, and one transparent virtual-companionship proposition.

The recommended launch sequence is:

1. Complete and review the Arfa prototype.
2. Conduct customer, legal, payment, and policy validation.
3. Build Beta on the production architecture.
4. Run a controlled cloud deployment rehearsal.
5. Invite 100 to 300 pilot customers.
6. Expand only after trust, retention, economics, and safety gates pass.

Primary strategic condition:

> Paid public launch requires clear disclosure that virtual profiles are not
> independent real members.

## 2. Company Purpose

Mission:

> Help adults find respectful conversation and connection with less pressure,
> clearer expectations, and stronger customer control.

Product principles:

- Mobile first
- Adults only
- Clear identity and disclosure
- Consent and customer control
- Transparent cost
- Respectful conversation
- Privacy by design
- Measured, reversible growth

## 3. Customer Problem

Customers experience:

- Slow or absent responses
- Swipe fatigue
- Scams and uncertain profile authenticity
- Unwanted explicit content
- Anxiety about beginning or continuing conversation
- Pricing and subscription frustration
- Poor support after a problem

DatingEasy888 proposes two related solutions:

- Real-member discovery with honest profile status and safety controls
- Immediately available virtual conversation for customers who knowingly choose it

## 4. Product Model

### Customer Experience

The customer application provides:

- Registration, age gate, login, and profile management
- Discovery, search, favorites, and chat history
- Real-time text, photo, voice, and approved gifts
- Blocking, reporting, privacy, and account controls
- Credit balance and transaction history
- Clear profile identity: `Real Member` or `Virtual Profile`

The customer product remains mobile-first web through Arfa and Beta. Native iOS
and Android applications follow after the web workflows and APIs are stable.
After launch, customers may choose the installed application or continue using
the complete service in a mobile browser. The website remains a first-class
acquisition and usage channel, and no core workflow requires installation.

### Virtual Conversation

Employee-operated and robot profiles may:

- Converse only with real customers
- Use prepared text and approved AI assistance
- Maintain conversation context
- Follow time, safety, and workload limits

They may not:

- Pretend to be an independent real person
- Promise an offline meeting
- Pressure a customer to spend
- Exploit emotional dependency
- Circumvent content or spending controls

### Company Operations

The company uses separate employee, administrator, and CEO interfaces for:

- Conversation operations
- Customer and employee account support
- Policy maintenance
- Robot scheduling and monitoring
- Financial reporting and approvals
- Security, health, and incident response

## 5. Target Market

Initial research hypothesis:

- U.S. adults approximately 35 to 55
- Mobile-web users
- Seeking conversation, companionship, or dating
- Comfortable with transparent virtual companionship
- Prefer a lower-pressure experience

Real-member discovery requires local density. The pilot should choose one
metropolitan area after comparing customer demand and acquisition cost in
three candidates. Los Angeles can be one candidate because it is operationally
convenient, but it is not assumed to be the winner.

The service must not target people based on a sensitive emotional or health
condition, and marketing must not exploit loneliness, grief, disability, or
financial vulnerability.

## 6. Competitive Strategy

DatingEasy888 will not win by having more profiles than major dating products.
It can compete on:

- Immediate conversation availability
- Transparent choice between real and virtual experiences
- Simple mobile operation
- Conversation quality and continuity
- Safety and spending controls
- Responsive customer support

The differentiation disappears if virtual profiles are hidden. Honest identity
is therefore both a compliance requirement and a brand advantage.

## 7. Brand Strategy

Working brand: `DatingEasy888`.

Before public use:

- Check domain ownership and alternatives.
- Conduct trademark and confusing-similarity review.
- Test whether `888` helps trust or makes the brand look promotional.
- Test `DatingEasy` against names emphasizing conversation or companionship.
- Reserve consistent social and app-store names.

Public language should emphasize social connection, companionship, and dating.
Avoid presenting the service as pornographic, escort-related, compensated
dating, or sexual entertainment.

## 8. Revenue Model

### Current Credit Model

- Registration reward: 50 credits
- Text: 5 credits
- Image or voice: 10 credits
- Gifts: 100 to 10,000 credits
- Credit packages: $10 to $100 and above

This model should remain a prototype test cell, not the only commercial plan.

### Recommended Hybrid Test

Test three concepts:

1. Credits only
2. Monthly membership plus discounted credits
3. Membership for real-member features, credits for virtual sessions and gifts

Possible membership value:

- Expanded real-member messaging
- Search and privacy controls
- Read status and advanced filters
- Monthly virtual-conversation allowance

Rules:

- Show exact price before every purchase.
- Explain whether a chat is with a real or virtual profile before spending.
- Do not describe credits as cash or withdrawable money.
- Make cancellation and account closure simple.
- Do not activate high-value gifts in the pilot.

## 9. Employee Economics

The current 30% attributed-revenue reward is financially and behaviorally risky.
It can create an incentive to prolong paid conversations or encourage gifts.

Recommended operating compensation:

- Stable hourly or salary compensation
- Response-quality bonus
- Safety and policy-compliance bonus
- Customer-satisfaction component
- Capped team-level service bonus

No employee should benefit directly from one customer's gift or unusual spending.
Robot performance should be evaluated on quality, safety, and customer choice,
not revenue extraction.

## 10. Go-To-Market Plan

### Stage A: Discovery

Duration: 4 to 6 weeks.

- Interview 30 potential customers.
- Survey 200 qualified adults.
- Test three transparent landing pages.
- Test brand names and disclosure language.
- Demonstrate the mobile prototype.
- Request preliminary payment-provider underwriting.
- Engage qualified legal and privacy review.

Decision:
- Continue only if customers understand and accept the proposition.

### Stage B: Waitlist And Community

- Publish useful content about safe dating and better conversation.
- Build an opt-in waitlist in the selected geography.
- Recruit small community and creator partners.
- Provide honest demonstrations of real and virtual experiences.
- Measure registration intent without fake scarcity or fake activity.

### Stage C: Closed Pilot

- Invite 100 to 300 customers.
- Set low daily and monthly spend limits.
- Keep the Big Rocket disabled.
- Review robot and employee conversations.
- Provide rapid human support.
- Reconcile payments, credits, gifts, and reports every day.

### Stage D: Controlled Growth

- Add certified Google Search advertising.
- Add referral incentives with identity and fraud controls.
- Expand content and community partnerships.
- Increase geography only when real-member density remains useful.
- Release native applications after app-store readiness review.

## 11. Marketing Experiments

### Concept Tests

Test these messages with full disclosure:

- "Real people and virtual conversation, with clear choices."
- "An easier way to begin a respectful conversation."
- "Social connection without the pressure to swipe endlessly."

Do not make claims such as:

- "Thousands of local singles waiting for you" unless independently verified
- "Guaranteed love"
- "A real person always replies" when virtual profiles may reply
- "Free" when meaningful use requires payment

### Channel Measurements

| Channel | Primary measure | Stop condition |
|---|---|---|
| Waitlist page | Qualified registration rate | Below 5% after message iteration |
| Customer interviews | Disclosure comprehension | Below 90% |
| Organic search | Activated users, not traffic alone | Low profile completion |
| Creator/community | Cost per activated user | Poor safety or brand fit |
| Referral | Activated referred customer | Fraud or incentive abuse |
| Paid search | Contribution CAC and payback | LTV/CAC below 3 |

## 12. Operating Model

Initial functional ownership:

- CEO: strategy, funding, high-risk spending, and final release approval
- Product: customer research, requirements, and measurement
- Engineering: web, services, data, robot engine, cloud, and reliability
- Trust and safety: moderation, incidents, policy, and training
- Operations: employee staffing, quality, schedules, and support
- Finance: reconciliation, chargebacks, reporting, and controls
- Legal/privacy: terms, consent, advertising, data, and regulatory review
- Marketing: research, content, partnerships, and approved acquisition

The same person may initially hold several roles, but financial approval,
production access, and reconciliation should not be controlled by one person.

## 13. Trust, Safety, And Compliance

Required before real-customer pilot:

- Clear real/virtual profile disclosure
- 18+ age gate and age-assurance plan
- Terms, privacy notice, content policy, and refund policy
- Blocking, reporting, moderation, and appeals
- AI output controls and human escalation
- Consent controls for mature content
- Simple cancellation and account deletion
- Data retention and deletion schedule
- Payment-provider approval
- Advertising certification
- Security and incident-response review

App-store publication additionally requires:

- Mature-content gating
- UGC filtering and reporting
- Customer blocking
- Public support contact
- In-app purchase compliance where applicable
- High-quality, differentiated native experience

## 14. Financial Planning Model

These figures are planning assumptions, not revenue forecasts.

### Monthly Scenarios

| Scenario | MAU | Payer rate | Monthly ARPPU | Gross revenue |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Validation pilot | 2,000 | 4% | $30 | $2,400 |
| Early city fit | 20,000 | 5% | $35 | $35,000 |
| Initial scale | 100,000 | 6% | $40 | $240,000 |

Conservative variable-cost assumptions under the current model:

- App distribution and payment: 15%
- Refunds, chargebacks, fraud, and reserves: 4%
- Transactional AI and cloud: 3%
- Employee reward: 30%

Total: 52% of gross revenue.
Contribution before fixed operating cost: 48%.

| Scenario | Gross revenue | Contribution at 48% |
|---|---:|---:|
| Validation pilot | $2,400 | $1,152 |
| Early city fit | $35,000 | $16,800 |
| Initial scale | $240,000 | $115,200 |

At $100,000 monthly fixed operating cost, 48% contribution requires about
$208,333 in monthly gross revenue to break even. At 6% payer conversion and
$40 ARPPU, this is approximately 86,806 monthly active users.

If the blended employee incentive falls from 30% to 10%, contribution rises to
approximately 68%. The same fixed-cost assumption then requires about $147,059
in monthly gross revenue, or approximately 61,275 monthly active users at the
same payer rate and ARPPU.

### Customer Acquisition Constraint

Example:

- $35 monthly ARPPU
- Four paid months
- 50% contribution margin

Contribution lifetime value per payer is about $70. At 5% payer conversion,
contribution value per activated customer is only $3.50 before fixed costs.
Paid acquisition would be difficult at that level.

The business must improve at least some of:

- Retention
- Payer conversion
- Contribution margin
- Organic and referral acquisition
- Membership revenue
- Employee operating efficiency

### Pre-Public Planning Envelope

Broad planning range:

| Stage | Estimated planning range |
|---|---:|
| Market, legal, payment, and policy validation | $25,000 to $75,000 |
| Beta engineering, security, and cloud foundation | $300,000 to $800,000 |
| Six-month closed-pilot runway | $250,000 to $600,000 |
| Total before broader public launch | $575,000 to $1,475,000 |

Actual cost depends heavily on internal staffing, contractor rates, AI usage,
legal scope, insurance, support hours, and geography. Vendor quotes and a
bottom-up staffing plan must replace these ranges before financing decisions.

## 16. Milestones And Gates

### Gate 1: Arfa Review

- Agreed workflows work on mobile and desktop.
- Prototype tests pass.
- Known limitations are documented.

### Gate 2: Business Validation

- At least 90% disclosure comprehension.
- Customer interviews support a focused proposition.
- Legal and payment paths appear feasible.
- Pricing concept is selected for Beta testing.

### Gate 3: Beta Readiness

- Production architecture implemented.
- Security, privacy, audit, and recovery controls tested.
- Payment sandbox and reconciliation pass.
- Robot safety and concurrency tests pass.

### Gate 4: Closed Pilot

- Payment-provider approval obtained.
- No unresolved critical safety or security defect.
- Pilot spending limits configured.
- Customer support and incident coverage staffed.

### Gate 5: Controlled Public Launch

- Day-30 return at least 10%.
- Payer conversion at least 5%.
- Refund plus chargeback rate below 3%.
- LTV/CAC at least 3.
- Disclosure complaints below 1%.
- No systematic safety or identity misunderstanding.

## 17. Principal Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Customers mistake virtual profiles for real people | Clear labels at every decision and payment point |
| Payment provider rejects model | Seek underwriting before major build or marketing spend |
| App stores reject sexual or low-quality dating app | Social/dating positioning, mature-content controls, distinctive native product |
| Employee incentives encourage spending pressure | Replace direct revenue commission with quality-based compensation |
| High gift spending creates harm or chargebacks | Remove Big Rocket, limits, cooling-off, unusual-spend review |
| Real-member network is too thin | Launch in one city and measure local liquidity |
| AI produces unsafe or manipulative content | Policy engine, context limits, human escalation, logging, kill switch |
| Customer acquisition is too expensive | Organic content, referrals, partnerships, retention and membership tests |
| Brand lacks trust | Name testing, clear ownership, support visibility, security and privacy proof |
